01 ‚Äî The Foundation

Perth was always the starting point ‚Äî never the limitation.

Growing up in Perth gave me a belief that geography is no longer destiny. I took that belief global ‚Äî studying at Murdoch, then Oxford, MIT, and HKU ‚Äî building frameworks, networks, and perspectives that most Perth founders never had access to.

Winning a Churchill Fellowship in 2014 to study innovation ecosystems around the world was a turning point. It proved that the model Perth needed already existed somewhere else. It just needed someone to come home and build it.

02 ‚Äî The Build

Spacecubed wasn't just a co-working space. It was a bet on Perth.

I founded Spacecubed to solve a problem I experienced firsthand: brilliant people in Perth with no community around them, no access to capital, no connective tissue between ideas and execution. Twelve years later, Spacecubed has become the heartbeat of Perth's startup ecosystem ‚Äî supporting over 1,500 founders, incubating ideas that have scaled globally, and proving that world-class innovation can be built from the west coast of Australia.

CORE Innovation Hub followed ‚Äî extending the same philosophy to social enterprise, where the need for community and capital infrastructure is even greater. Both are built on the same conviction: the ecosystem is the product.

The ecosystem is maturing. Time to go bigger.

Spacecubed 3.0 is about scale ‚Äî a national footprint, deeper capital connections, and programs that compete with the world's best. The AsiaGlobal Fellowship at HKU in 2025 put me in rooms with the next generation of Asia-Pacific leaders and opened up venture and partnership opportunities that Perth alone couldn't access.

Through BGLV and Purpose Ventures, I'm now backing and building the next generation of founders ‚Äî bringing everything I've learned about ecosystems, community, and capital to bear on ventures that can scale well beyond Australia.
